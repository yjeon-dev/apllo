1. NASA's Apollo Plan directly aimed at the Soviet Union

[List]

U.S. President John F. Kennedy announced his great aspirations on May 25, 1961.







“American enterprise--time for this nation to take a leading role in space achievement,
which in many ways may hold the key to our future on Earth. …

First, I believe that this nation should commit itself to achieve the goal, before this decade is out,
of landing a man on the moon and returning him safely to the Earth.”
John F. Kennedy

The lunar exploration was a challenging goal, but as the United States was entirely behind the Soviet Union in the space exploration race, President Kennedy began looking for a space exploration goal that would impress people worldwide.




2. Preparations for the success of the Apollo Plan are underway

[List]

When the spacecraft was first developed, NASA considered three methods.
'Direct Ascent', 'Earth Orbit Rendezvous', and 'Lunar Orbit Rendezvous'.
The 'Lunar Orbit Rendezvous' method was finally selected in consideration of cost and technical survivability.

Project Mercury(link), a one astronaut embarkation, and Project Gemini(link), a two-man embark, were started, and repeated training such as space rendezvous, docking was conducted.

In December 1968, the United States made the Apollo 8 round-trip to the moon.

In 1969, the Apollo 9 was used to succeed in docking in the Earth's atmosphere.

On May 18, 1969, Apollo 10 was launched, and the final test was conducted for a lunar landing. The Apollo 10 mission encompassed all aspects of an actual crewed lunar landing, except the landing.

Before Apollo 11, there was a wide variety of activities.




3. Apollo 11 successfully landed on the Moon

[List]


On July 16, 1969, the Apollo 11, carrying three astronauts, including Captain Neil Armstrong, Edwin Aldrin, Jr, and Michael Collins, was launched toward the Moon.


Apollo 11 was launched by a Saturn V rocket from Kennedy Space Center on Merritt Island, Florida.




5,  4,  3,  2,  1,  0...




Apollo 11 launched towards the moon.






And on July 20, the Apollo 11 LM"Eagle" was the first crewed vehicle to land on the Moon.

"Houston, Tranquility Base here … the Eagle has landed," Neil Armstrong




After about six and a half hours, Neil Armstrong put his left foot on the rocky Moon. It was the first human footprint on the Moon.



4. Neil Armstrong, Edwin Aldrin, explore the moon surface

[List]

"That's one small step for a man, one giant leap for mankind."
Neil Armstrong



Collecting samples, and taking photographs. Rock and soil samples were collected, and seismometer and observation equipment were installed. The way the two walked was like dancing slowly.


Because of the small gravity, the rising body does not easily come down to the surface of the moon. They left behind an American flag and revealed a plaque with the following text in the text below.


"Here men from the planet Earth first set foot upon the moon, July 1969 A.D.
We came in peace for all mankind."

They explore the surface for two and a half hours.




5. Apollo 11, Return to Earth

[List]

Armstrong and Aldrin take off from the moon on July 21.
They fired an ascending rocket and left the lunar surface, and docked with the mothership orbiting the moon.
Armstrong and Aldrin met again with Collins in Columbia. The CSM ignites the engine to enter its path towards Earth.

After two and a half days of sailing to Earth, Apollo 11 arrives at Earth.
Columbia enters the Earth's atmosphere and lands safely on July 24.

Later, the astronauts were transported by helicopter to the USS Hornet and entered a mobile containment facility.
After three weeks of quarantine, Armstrong, Collins, and Aldrin were greeted with parades worldwide.






6. The giant leap for humankind

[List]

With Apollo 11, the United States demonstrated human science and technology capabilities in the 20th century and changed social perceptions of space.
With the success of Apollo 11, humankind has entered the era of full-scale science and technology and can leap beyond the Earth to a distant universe.
